About

When the deadline for a huge project is right around the corner, you may need a big crew to finish on time. The rest of the time, having one painter working alone on your project has a lot of advantages. Here are a few:

  • Keeps Costs Low

    With smaller operations, it's easier to keep costs low (a lot of the time). We both save money this way.

  • Increased Accountability

    Each new addition to a team will have more of a challenge in being efficient than the last guy. This can lead to miscommunication, unaligned standards, and a chaotic environment that leads to mistakes.

  • Consistent Results

    Having one person see the project through from start to finish is the best way to make sure that everything ends up with a consistent, clean, uniform look.

  • More Organized

    With one person using the tools, there is no need for excess and it's easier to keep track of where everything is, keeping things efficient.

  • Quiet and nondisruptive

    While crews are fast, they can also be quite intrusive to your space. With one person paiting, often you forget they're even there.

Don't pay top dollar for big brands to send inexperienced painters. Trust someone devoted to their craft.
